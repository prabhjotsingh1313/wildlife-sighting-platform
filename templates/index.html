The Queensland Glider Network (QGN) was established in early 2006 to support glider populations by being a statewide hub for communication, education, data collection, mapping, and information exchange.

Our Aims

Australia and parts of South-East Asia are home to the worldâ€™s only gliding marsupials. The smallest glider species, the feathertail glider (Acrobates pygmaeus), and the largest, the southern and central greater glider (Petauroides volans), both rely on the eucalypt forests and woodlands of eastern Australia.

Vegetation clearing, along with climate change-induced increases in bushfires, has driven declines in all glider species. However, greater gliders and yellow-bellied gliders have suffered the most. Both are now threatened under Commonwealth and Queensland legislation.

The goal of the QGN is to raise awareness of gliders and their habitat requirements across Queensland. We aim to:

  • Promote glider-friendly landscape management
  • Enhance habitat within remnant glider populations
  • Implement monitoring programs for local glider populations
  • Work with governments to implement conservation projects
  • Educate communities via workshops, webinars, and spotlighting events
  • Partner with landcare and rehabilitation groups
